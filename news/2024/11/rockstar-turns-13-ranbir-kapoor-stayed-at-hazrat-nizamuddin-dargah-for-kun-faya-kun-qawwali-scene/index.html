Rockstar Turns 13: Ranbir Kapoor Stayed At Hazrat Nizamuddin Dargah For Kun Faya Kun Qawwali Scene

Rockstar completes 13 years today.

There are several ways the movie and its music changed Indian cinema.

The most fascinating narrative that hangs out from the making of the film is that of Ranbir Kapoor, who went out of the way to embody Janardan Jakhar.

This transformative experience includes his stay at the Hazrat Nizamuddin Dargah in Delhi.

For the qawwali scene in the song Kun Faya Kun, Kapoor stayed for nearly two days in Hazrat Nizamuddin Dargah.

Rich in spiritual atmosphere and vivid qawwali performances, this shrine allowed Kapoor to receive insight into the cultural and spiritual components reflected in the film.

During his stay, Ranbir Kapoor was able to relate to the spirit behind the song, which in fact pursues the divine purpose.

In a 2011 interview with the Hindustan Times, Ranbir himself called the experience “extremely spiritual”.

Kun Faya Kun, produced by A.

R.

Rahman with lyrics from Irshad Kamil, became one of the most appreciated tracks of the Rockstar album.

Initially, there were concerns regarding the religious tone of this song, Imtiaz Ali, who directed the movie, told Ranveer Allahbadia on his podcast.

But the trio – Imtiaz Ali, A.

R.

Rahman and Irshad Kamil – decided to include it in the movie after due consultations concerning the fact that it was sensitive.

It was sung by Mohit Chauhan, Javed Ali and A R Rahman himself contributed to it.

The dynamics of the lyrics and musical tune of Rockstar have made it a cult classic.

Meanwhile, Kapoor’s devotion was not restricted to his stay at the Dargah.

He also went to live with a Jat family for several days so that he could better understand how grounded his character was.

Such deep involvement enabled him to get a richer insight into the details of Janardan Jakhar’s opportunities and challenges, reports Pink Villa.

More so, Kapoor worked very hard to master the guitar in order to look real on the screen.
