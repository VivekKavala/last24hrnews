Kim Hyun Jin Roped In For K-Drama Bunny And Her Boys? Find Out

Korean star Kim Hyun Jin has joined the cast of the upcoming rom-com Bunny and Her Boys (working title).

Previously, South Korean media confirmed that Hierarchy stars Roh Jeong Eui and Lee Chae Min, along with All That We Loved actor Jo Joon Young, will also feature in the K-drama, which is based on a popular webtoon of the same name.

On November 4, Korean news outlet NewSen reported that Kim Hyun Jin is set to join the stellar cast of Bunny and Her Boys as a talented sculptor named Jo Ah Rang.

He is a renowned name in the art world, and his good looks and playful personality have earned him the title of the “idol of the art world.

” The 28-year-old actor is best known for his recent role as Joo Il Young in My Sweet Mobster and Jin Sun Ho in the 2022 rom-com Cheer Up.

Fans are excited to see Kim Hyun Jin mould himself into the character of Jo Ah Rang in the upcoming drama Bunny and Her Boys.

Moreover, during the 2024 Asia Model Awards, he bagged the Rising Star award, adding to his other accolades, which include the Best New Actor award from the 2022 SBS Drama Awards.

Meanwhile, Roh Jeong Eui will play the role of Ban Hee Jin, aka Bunny, a top student at Yein University’s sculpture department.

Lee Chae Min will star as Hwang Jae Yeol, a driven and compassionate student from the Visual Design Department who dreams of becoming an art director.

Whereas, Jo Joon Young will mould himself into Cha Ji Won, a charismatic, high-achieving student in the top 0.

1 per cent academically and the grandson of a wealthy family.

The storyline of the upcoming romantic comedy will follow Ban Hee Jin’s life as she navigates her journey of growth, relationships, and self-discovery.

Set in the vibrant atmosphere of a university, Bunny and Her Boys will showcase how Ban Hee Jin experiences heartbreak due to her first love, which leaves her questioning everything.

She eventually earns the title of “love fool.

” Following this, she tries to rebuild her confidence.

Many attractive men enter her life, posing new challenges.

K-drama enthusiasts are waiting with their bated breath for the story of Bunny and Her Friends to unfold.

According to reports, the rom-com might premiere in the first half of 2025 through South Korea’s one of the leading channels, MBC.
