Smart energy management systems can help real estate developers cut electricity costs by 20%: Legrand India’s Tony Berland

As the country focuses on achieving its net zero 2070 goals, it is keeping a strong focus on the energy sector and increasing efforts around green real estate.

This provides opportunities for manufacturers of household appliances and electrical products to introduce energy efficient devices.

This is where Group Legrand India wants to tap on.

In a conversation with ET Digital, Tony Berland, MD & CEO, Group Legrand India, talks about how energy-efficient home appliances are paving the way for sustainability in real estate.

Edited excerpts:Economic Times (ET): How do you see India’s real estate sector adapting to sustainability? What needs to be done to push this?Tony Berland (TB): Sustainability in the Indian real estate market is gaining momentum, as seen in the growing adoption of green building certifications and energy-efficient trends.

One notable example is the increasing popularity of Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design (LEED) certified buildings, which place a strong emphasis on energy efficiency and reducing operational costs.

However, the sector still has significant ground to cover to fully embrace sustainable practices.

For instance, the use of sustainable construction materials and energy-efficient electrical installations can reduce energy consumption by up to 30%.

We are already addressing this shift by offering smart lighting solutions and energy-saving electrical products for both commercial and residential buildings.

By implementing intelligent energy management systems, real estate developers can cut electricity costs by as much as 20%, making sustainability not only environmentally beneficial but also economically profitable.

Our environment commitment includes providing our customers with eco-designed products and services that enable them to reduce their energy consumption.

For each product family, we draw up an EPD (environmental product declaration) or PEP (product environmental profile) — a declaration that is like an environment photograph of the product.

It is based on the ISO 14025 international standard and a multi-criteria life cycle analysis.

It is a recognised and reliable programme with international scopePEP is a significant contributor to the group’s green building policy.

Informing users about the environmental impact of the products they use is our top priority.

ET: As a company that manufactures electrical products for homes and buildings.

Is Legrand India also working on building products which will help in energy efficiency?TB: Yes, one notable advancement is our smart lighting systems, which reduce energy consumption by 30-40% through automation and daylight sensors.

These systems adapt to natural light levels and optimise usage, making them highly efficient.

Our building management systems (BMS) are deployed in numerous high-rise offices to manage lighting, heating, ventilation, air conditioning (HVAC), and other electrical loads based on real-time temperature conditions.

These systems enable up to 20% savings in energy costs, making them an essential solution for modern infrastructure.

We are also proud of our energy-efficient designs for switches and sockets, which help households reduce electricity consumption.

Additionally, our smart meters have revolutionised energy management, allowing users to monitor and track their real-time energy usage, empowering them to make informed decisions about consumption.

ET: How has Legrand India adopted sustainability in its supply chain and manufacturing?TB: With respect to supply chain, from route optimisation to load capacity enhancement and deploying EV/CNG vehicles, our supply chain team is committed to reducing carbon footprint.

And, through such various best practices, we have reduced approximately 20% CO2 emission in our downstream transportation since 2021.

We also work with our suppliers, sensitising, training and supporting them to commit to our sustainability goals.

Our purchase team works closely to identify suppliers who share our commitment to develop a circular economy, and work with recyclers with EPR certification, ensuring right recycling procedures.

We have implemented efficient practices at all our manufacturing sites, cutting down our waste generation by 500 tonnes with 99% recovery rate.

Through our water conservation efforts, we have recycled 5,600 KL of water through after treatment use.

Our commitment to sustainability extends to packaging as well, with 93% of our packaging being eco-friendly.

There has been a 10% reduction in the weight of our products over the last few months.

We are actively eliminating single-use plastics from our packaging and incorporating sustainable packaging.

To further reduce our environmental footprint, we regularly conduct energy audits across all our sites.

These audits have resulted in reducing energy costs by more than 25% compared with 2021.

Our inhouse innovative digital platform for environmental data reporting empowers us to track specific environmental parameters like energy usage, waste management, water management and emissions with great precision.

ET: What are the sustainability targets and goals of Legrand India?TB: We aim to reduce CO2 emissions across our supply chain by 30% by 2030.

We have set a target to source 70% of our energy from renewable sources by 2024 and to achieve 85% by 2027.

In line with our commitment to social responsibility, we have facilitated 500 KW of renewable energy at 20 institutions through various community projects, actively involving local populations in the transition to sustainable energy.
