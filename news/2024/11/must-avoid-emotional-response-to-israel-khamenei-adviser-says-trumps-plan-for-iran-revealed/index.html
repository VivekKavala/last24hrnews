Must Avoid 'Emotional response' To Israel, Khamenei Adviser Says, Trump's Plan For Iran Revealed?

An adviser to Iran's supreme leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei has warned against an "instinctive" response to Israel.

Israeli warplanes struck military sites in Iran on Oct.

26 in retaliation for a large Iranian missile attack on Israel at the start of the month.

"Israel aims to bring the conflict to Iran.

We must act wisely to avoid its trap and not react instinctively," the adviser, Ali Larijani, told state television.

Iran fired 200 missiles at Israel on Oct.

1 in response to the killing of Hezbollah chief Hassan Nasrallah and Hamas leader Ismail Haniyeh.

After Israel hit back, it warned Iran against any counterattack, but the Islamic republic has vowed to respond.
