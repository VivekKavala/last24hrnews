‘Really Good Night For Putin’: Jimmy Kimmel On Donald Trump’s Win

Jimmy Kimmel became emotional after the US presidential election results were announced.

The television personality and producer described it as a sad day for the country, including those who voted for US-elected President Donald Trump.

In his speech, Jimmy not only expressed his disappointment with the choice made by the people of his country but also made a special request to Trump.

He jokingly asked the President to put him in a prison cell with Taylor Swift.

On his first late-night show monologue after Donald Trump defeated Kamala Harris for the presidency, Jimmy Kimmel appeared visibly disheartened.

The host noted, “Let’s be honest, it was a terrible night last night.

It was a terrible night for women, for children, for the hundreds of thousands of hard-working immigrants who make this country go, for health care, for our climate, for science, for journalism, for justice, for free speech.

It was a terrible night for poor people, for the middle class, for seniors who rely on Social Security, for our allies in Ukraine, for NATO and democracy and decency.

” The host even paused briefly, as he became emotional and later continued to finish his speech.

He mentioned that it was a terrible night not only for those who voted against him but also for the people who voted in his favour.

However, in his speech, Jimmy sarcastically mentioned that results would surely benefit Vladimir Putin and billionaires like Elon Musk and others who live in Silicon Valley who blindly support Donald Trump.

Moreover, referring to Donald Trump’s negative views of Taylor Swift and constant claims to put his opponent behind bars, Jimmy Kimmel made a hilarious request saying, “My only request to President Trump is that he let me share a prison cell with Taylor Swift.

I’m really good at making bracelets and I think we’d get along just fine.

” Donald Trump’s victory came after record-breaking results, allowing him a second round as the President of the United States of America.

He won after crossing the threshold of 270 votes over the Democrat candidate Kamala Harris, who had received endorsements from Taylor Swift, Beyonce, Eminem, Billie Eilish, and other celebrities.
