BIS Group A, B And C Online Exam Admit Card Released, Exam From November 19

The Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) has published the admit cards for candidates who applied for Group A, B and C positions.

Applicants can now access and download their admit cards directly from the official BIS website at bis.

gov.

in.

The call letter for the online examination will be accessible on the website until November 21.

This recruitment drive aims to fill a total of 345 vacancies across various positions, including essential roles such as Personal Assistant (PA), Assistant Director (AD) and Senior Secretariat Assistant (SSA).

Steps to Download BIS Group A, B And C Exam Admit Card Step 1: Go to the official BIS website at bis.

gov.

in.

Step 2: On the homepage, select the Careers link.

Step 3: This will open a new page where applicants should click on the BIS Admit Card 2024 link for Group A, B, and C positions.

Step 4: A new page will appear, prompting candidates to enter their login information.

Step 5: Press submit, and your admit card will appear on the screen.

Step 6: Review the details on the admit card, then download it.

Step 7: Take a printout of the copy for future reference.

BIS Group A, B And C Exam: Details To Check On The Admit Card — Name of Exam Conducting Body — Exam Title — Roll Number / Registration Number — Examination Centre Name and Address — Position Title — Date of Birth — Examination Venue — Venue Code — Examination Date — Reporting Time — Scanned Images of Photograph and Signature — Designated Space for Candidate’s Signature and Photograph BIS Group A, B And C Exam Schedule, Pattern: The examination is scheduled for November 19 and 21.

On November 19, the Assistant Section Officer exam will take place in Shift 1, from 8:30 am to 10:30 am.

Shift 2, held from 12:30 pm to 2:30 pm, will be for the Junior Secretariat Assistant and Personal Assistant posts.

Shift 3, running from 4:30 pm to 6:30 pm, will cover the Technical Assistant, Stenographer, and Assistant positions.

On November 21, the Senior Secretariat Assistant, Assistant Director, and Senior Technician exams will be conducted in Shift 3 from 4:30 pm to 6:30 pm.

The online exam will consist of 150 questions, each carrying one mark, with a total duration of 120 minutes.

Except for the English Language section, the question paper will be available in both English and Hindi.

Each question will have five options, out of which only one is correct.

Marks will be deducted for every incorrect answer; specifically, one-fourth of the marks for each question will be subtracted for each wrong answer given.
