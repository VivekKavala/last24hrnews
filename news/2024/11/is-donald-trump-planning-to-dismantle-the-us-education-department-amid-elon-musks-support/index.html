Is Donald Trump planning to dismantle the US Education Department amid Elon Musk’s support?

Amid ongoing discussions about reducing federal influence in education, former President Donald Trump has reignited his call to eliminate the US Department of Education, suggesting that shifting authority back to states could lead to a more effective system.

Trump’s proposal has received public support from billionaire Elon Musk, who endorsed the idea as “good,” reflecting a broader conservative push for minimized federal intervention in local educational affairs, as mentioned in a report by Forbes.

Trump’s Agenda: A State-Centric Approach to EducationTrump has long advocated for a decentralized education system, arguing that state governments should assume full responsibility for educational decisions and funding.

Established in 1979, the Department of Education was originally intended to manage education funding, enforce civil rights in schools, and coordinate federal education programs.

However, Trump views the department as an example of excessive federal oversight, contending that it has not yielded satisfactory returns for American taxpayers.

Congressional Hurdles for Education Department's EliminationEliminating the Department of Education would require substantial legislative support, including a 60-vote supermajority in the Senate.

Despite Republican gains in recent elections, their current Senate count remains short of the needed threshold.

Previous attempts to eliminate the department, including a 2023 House proposal, faced resistance from within the GOP, with prominent Republicans like Representative Elise Stefanik opposing the measure.

As such, experts caution that complete elimination may prove challenging, even with renewed efforts under a Trump administration.

What Would Happen to Federal Education Funding?One major question is the fate of federal funding, which currently constitutes about 14% of total education financing nationwide.

The Department of Education administers programs like Title I, which supports low-income K-12 schools, and manages funding for special education programs.

Critics argue this could undermine programs that benefit disadvantaged students, potentially widening the gap in educational access and quality.

Impact on Student Loans and Civil Rights EnforcementThe Department of Education currently oversees federal student loans, Pell Grants, and anti-discrimination policies under Title IX.

If the department were dismantled, these functions might be reassigned to agencies like the Department of Justice or Treasury.

Project 2025 proposes cuts to programs like Title I and Head Start, which provides early childhood education for low-income families, prompting concerns over increased educational inequality.

Civil rights enforcement, including protections against gender identity and sexual orientation discrimination, would likely also face significant changes under Trump’s agenda.

A Broader Conservative MovementCalls to eliminate the Department of Education are not new; Ronald Reagan, Bob Dole, and other prominent Republicans have championed similar causes.

The argument has consistently been centered on reducing federal influence over education, emphasizing state control.

According to a Pew Research survey, 44% of Americans have a favorable view of the Department of Education, with support much higher among Democrats than Republicans, reflecting deep partisan divides over the department’s role.
