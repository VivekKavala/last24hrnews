Haryana NMMSS Admit Card 2024 To Release Today; Check Steps To Download

The Board of School Education (BSEH) is set to release the admit cards for the Haryana National Means-cum-Merit Scholarship Scheme (NMMSS) Exam 2024 today, November 6.

Students appearing in this scholarship examination can access and download their admit cards from BSEH’s official website at bseh.

org.

in.

Additionally, the admit card link will also be provided on the official SCERT Haryana website at scertharyana.

gov.

in.

The Haryana NMMSS exam is scheduled to take place on November 17.

Haryana NMMSS Exam 2024: Steps to Download Admit Card Students can access and download their admit card by entering their Aadhaar number along with their date of birth.

Step 1: Go to the official BSEH website atbseh.

org.

in.

Step 2: On the homepage, find and click on the link for Haryana NMMSS Admit Card 2024.

Step 3: This will direct you to a new page where you’ll need to enter your login details.

Step 4: After entering the required information, click the ‘Submit’ button.

Step 5: Your admit card will appear on the screen; review all details carefully.

Step 6: Download and save the admit card.

Step 7: Take a printout or screenshot of the copy for future reference.

Haryana NMMSS Exam 2024: Exam Pattern The examination will consist of two sections: Part 1, the Mental Ability Test (MAT), and Part 2, the Scholastic Ability Test (SAT).

Part 1 will feature 90 multiple-choice questions, each worth one mark, focusing on topics such as reasoning, analytical skills, and synthesis.

Part 2 will also include 90 questions, covering subjects like Science, Mathematics, and Social Science.

Haryana NMMSS Exam 2024: Scholarship Amount The scholarship program offers financial assistance of Rs 12,000 per year to support students from grades 9 through 12.

Haryana NMMSS Exam 2024: Details Mentioned In Admit Card The Haryana NMMSS Admit Card 2024 will display the following details: -Name of the candidate -Father’s Name of the candidate -Candidate’s mother’s name -Registration number -Aadhar number -Date of Birth -Roll number -Reporting Time -Exam Timings -Exam centre Address -Exam centre code -Candidate’s photograph -Candidate’s signature -Exam Day Guidelines Candidates who are visually impaired or have a physical disability, with a certified disability of 40 percent or higher as indicated in a medical certificate issued by the Chief Medical Officer, qualify for the Board’s scribe assistance if they are unable to write independently.

Candidates interested in this service can regularly check the BSEH website for further details and updates.
