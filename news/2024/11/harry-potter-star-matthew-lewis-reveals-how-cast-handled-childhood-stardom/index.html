Harry Potter Star Matthew Lewis Reveals How Cast Handled Childhood Stardom

Imagine being a kid and stepping into a world filled with magic, fame and spells — a dream for many, but a real-life experience for Harry Potter star Matthew Lewis.

Now 35, the actor who brought Neville Longbottom to life reflected on why he and his co-stars including Daniel Radcliffe, Emma Watson and Rupert Grint, managed to escape the fate of many child stars who struggle as adults.

Spoiler alert: it wasn’t just because they had wands and cloaks.

Recently, in a Q&A session at Rhode Island Comic Con on November 3, Lewis shared that it was the cast’s strong friendships that kept them grounded, even with the immense fame that came with the wizarding world.

While discussing his decade-long journey on the Harry Potter set, Lewis didn’t hold back on the ups and downs they faced as young actors transitioning into adulthood.

“We’re very good at hiding all of those stories,” he joked, but then he got a bit more serious about the “tricky” reality of growing up in the industry.

He wasn’t entirely sure how he’d fit in as an adult actor.

“There was absolutely a lot of trepidation,” he said, adding that he worried if he’d be able to make it in the wider acting world.

He admitted, “After 10 years in Harry Potter, I was ready to do something else and also was not keen to do anything that was going to lead to multiple series.

” But it wasn’t all magic and charm spells.

Lewis explained that he and his castmates shared a unique bond that kept them sane amid the chaos of child stardom.

He believes their strong friendship helped them avoid the pitfalls that so often ensnare young actors.

“One of the questions that was asked earlier about the pitfalls of being a child actor that we seem to have avoided, I would posit that a large degree of that was because we were all there together,” he said.

Unlike many young actors who feel the weight of carrying a movie alone, Lewis and his co-stars had each other to lean on.

“A lot of the fears that we had were all shared,” he explained.

The tight-knit Harry Potter cast experienced both the highs and the challenges of their journey together, creating an unbreakable bond.

Lewis added that only they could understand what each other had gone through, “Anyone else in the world will never be able to understand really what we went through and how it affected our personal lives except each other.

Working together wasn’t just wonderful, it was lots of fun,” he added.

With Harry Potter set for a reboot on Max as a television series, fans are already curious if we’ll see Lewis return as Neville Longbottom.

For now, though, Lewis isn’t in a rush to dive back in.

“It’s not something I’m looking at or want to do but would not turn my nose up at,” he shared, noting that playing an adult Neville would be a fresh and intriguing angle.
