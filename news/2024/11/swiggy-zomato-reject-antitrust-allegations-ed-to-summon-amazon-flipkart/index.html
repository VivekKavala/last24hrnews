Swiggy, Zomato reject anti-trust allegations; ED to summon Amazon, Flipkart

Swiggy and Zomato have maintained that they are in compliance with India’s competition laws.

This and more in today’s ETtech Top 5.

Also in the letter:■ Amazon Seller Services cuts losses in FY24■ Trump effect on India’s iPhone output■ PLI targets slip away Zomato, Swiggy say firms in compliance with competition laws amid CCI probe 115183241Zomato CEO Deepinder Goyal; Sriharsha Majety, group CEO, SwiggyFood delivery firms Zomato and Swiggy, which have come under the scanner for potential antitrust violations, have rebuffed the claims.

Driving the news: Both the companies said they’re in compliance with India’s competition laws.

In a regulatory filing, Zomato said it will “promptly comply” with any recommendations from the Competition Commission of India (CCI), while Swiggy said it is yet to receive details of the CCI’s findings.

Word for word: “We will continue to work closely with the commission (CCI) to explain why all our practices are in compliance with the Competition Act,” Zomato said.

“Currently, it’s at a preliminary stage and no final decision or order has been issued regarding Swiggy's practices since 2022.

Swiggy is fully cooperating with the investigation and is committed to complying with the extant laws of the country,” the company said in a statement.

What are the allegations: Zomato reportedly entered into “exclusivity contracts” with partners in return for lower commissions and imposed pricing and discount restrictions on restaurant partners.

Swiggy allegedly threatened partner restaurants with reduced rankings if they did not maintain price parity.

It also guaranteed business growth to certain partners who listed exclusively on its platform.

Background: It was reported on November 8 that a CCI investigation found the food delivery giants breached competition laws, with their business practices favoring select restaurants listed on their platforms.

This pertains to an investigation that began in 2022 after a complaint by the National Restaurant Association of India (NRAI) about the impact on food outlets of alleged anti-competitive practices of the platforms.
