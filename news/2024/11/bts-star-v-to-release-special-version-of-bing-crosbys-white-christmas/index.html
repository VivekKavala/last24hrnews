BTS Star V To Release Special Version Of Bing Crosby's 'White Christmas'

BTS star Kim Taehyung aka V is ready with a surprise gift this Christmas.

In a special collaboration, V will release a new version of Bing Crosby’s timeless classic White Christmas, more than 80 years since its debut, his agency announced on Wednesday.

The 28-year-old K-pop sensation will join the legendary singer posthumously on a new remix of the 1942 hit, which is set for release on December 6.

This collaboration, which is a partnership among the Bing Crosby Estate, Primary Wave Music and Geffen Records, is said to be an “era-bridging” project.

To be released as a duet, the song comes almost 47 years after Crosby’s last duet – his 1977 collaboration with David Bowie on “Peace on Earth/Little Drummer Boy.

” View this post on Instagram A post shared by Bing Crosby (@bingcrosby) Speaking up about the collaboration, V shared that he feels very grateful to have had the opportunity to be featured in a song by his all-time favorite jazz artist.

“I grew up listening to his song ‘It’s Been a Long, Long Time’ on repeat, so it feels like such an honor to sing alongside the voice of someone I admire so much on White Christmas.

As a huge fan of his, I sang with deep respect and admiration.

And I hope the listeners will enjoy the song as much as I did while recording it.

Wishing everyone a wonderful holiday season!” he said in a statement.

For those unaware, the song, originally recorded for the 1942 film Holiday Inn, was later featured prominently in the 1954 movie White Christmas.

One of Crosby’s beloved tracks, it is still regarded as the best-selling single of all time.

Crosby passed away in 1977.

More On V’s Music Plans The collaborative song will arrive almost months after the release of V’s solo single “FRI(END)S” and his duet, Wherever U R.

The K-pop star is currently serving his mandatory military service in South Korea alongside four of his BTS bandmates, SUGA, RM, Jimin and Jung Kook.

The other eldest members, Jin and J-Hope, were recently discharged from the military.

As suggested by HYBE, the entire group is expected to reunite in 2025 and resume future activities in 2026.
