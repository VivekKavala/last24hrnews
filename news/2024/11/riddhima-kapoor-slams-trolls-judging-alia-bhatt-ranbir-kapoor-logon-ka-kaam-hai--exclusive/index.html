Riddhima Kapoor Slams Trolls 'Judging' Alia Bhatt, Ranbir Kapoor: 'Logon Ka Kaam Hai...' | Exclusive

Riddhima Kapoor Sahni has opened up about the continuous trolling and judgment her family faces, specifically targeting her brother Ranbir Kapoor and sister-in-law Alia Bhatt.

Ranbir’s remarks are frequently misconstrued in interviews, leading to misunderstandings.

Recently, Alia faced a wave of backlash due to rumours about alleged cosmetic procedures gone wrong.

A video surfaced claiming she had undergone an unsuccessful treatment, even going as far as to suggest her face was partially paralysed.

Alia responded to these unfounded rumours on Instagram, calling them “baseless” and expressing frustration with such invasive scrutiny.

In an exclusive conversation with News18 Showsha, Riddhima, who has been receiving rave reviews for her onscreen debut in Fabulous Lives vs Bollywood Wives, opened up about the toll these constant judgments can take on her family.

“There are judgments everywhere,” she said.

“People always find something to say, especially when it comes to families like ours.

People assume it’s all about privilege, but they don’t see what we go through.

I am proud and secure in my life.

I have a beautiful family, a supportive husband, a lovely daughter, a brother, a sister-in-law.

And I’m not apologetic for that.

” Reflecting on her views of privilege, Riddhima shared a personal perspective: “Privilege to me is having a family that loves you and being content with what you have.

And I don’t really care about what people have to say about that.

People will always find a reason to talk.

Logon ka kaam hai bolna, bolenge.

They’ll judge because it’s easy to criticise.

When you make a mistake, ten people jump on you immediately, but do they applaud when you do something good? Rarely.

” Riddhima spoke about how online culture has become unkind, feeding on negativity.

“We’re surrounded by so much negativity these days; it’s like a cycle.

The smallest slip-up, and they’re ready to pounce.

We need to change that culture.

” Alia also addressed the controversy on her Instagram, clarifying her stance, “Absolutely NO JUDGEMENT towards anyone who chooses cosmetic corrections or surgery – your body, your choice.

But wow, this is beyond ridiculous!” She voiced concern about the impact of such baseless stories, especially on younger audiences, who might be misled by these rumours.

“What’s worse, you’re influencing young, impressionable minds who might actually believe this garbage,” she added.
